Bill 16

Complete guide to Bill 16 and the management of divided co-ownerships in Quebec.

Update: March 2026 – Based on the Regulation establishing various rules regarding divided co-ownership, in force since August 14, 2025.

Bill 16, officially Bill no. 16 (2019, chapter 28), is the most significant reform of divided co-ownership in Quebec since the Civil Code of Quebec came into force in 1994. It aims to modernize the management of condominium corporations, strengthen the financial protection of unit owners, and ensure the long-term sustainability of Quebec's condominium building stock.

The implementing regulation (published July 30, 2025 in the Quebec Official Gazette) came into force on August 14, 2025. It puts in place the three main tools introduced by Bill 16: the maintenance log, the contingency fund study and the attestation on the state of the co-ownership. These obligations apply to all divided co-ownerships in Quebec, without exception.

1. Why Bill 16? The objectives of the reform

  • Shift from reactive management to proactive, 25-year planned management.
  • Protect unit owners against undercapitalized contingency funds.
  • Improve transparency at unit sales.
  • Strengthen the governance and accountability of boards of directors.
  • Ensure the durability of buildings (roofs, elevators, mechanical systems, etc.).

The reform follows in the wake of Bill no. 31 (2024) and primarily amends articles 1068.1, 1070.2, 1071 and 1070 of the Civil Code of Quebec (C.C.Q.).

2. The three mandatory pillars of Bill 16

The maintenance log (art. 1070.2 C.C.Q.)

Objective: Document the building's condition, plan future work, and maintain a history of interventions.

Mandatory content:

  • Detailed inventory of common areas (roof, elevators, foundations, heating systems, etc.) and private portions the corporation is responsible for.
  • For each element: installation date (if known), required maintenance (frequency), completed repairs, contracts, warranties, manuals, inspection reports.
  • Planning section: current condition, remaining lifespan, list of major repairs and replacements planned over at least 25 years (with estimated year).
  • Attached documents: plans, specifications, invoices, contracts.

Who can prepare or revise it?

An independent professional who is a member of the Order of Engineers, Certified Appraisers, Architects, or Professional Technologists of Quebec, whose primary activity relates to building management, construction, renovation, appraisal or inspection. Conflicts of interest are prohibited.

Updates and revisions:

  • Annual update by the board of directors.
  • Full revision by a professional every 5 years (or 10 years for small condominiums: ≤ 8 private units, no building or ≤ 3 above-ground storeys).
  • Physical inspection of the building is mandatory during production or revision.

⏰ Compliance deadline

  • Existing condominiums: no later than August 15, 2028.
  • New condominiums: provided by the developer no later than 30 days after the transition meeting.

The contingency fund study (art. 1071 C.C.Q.)

Objective: Determine the amount needed to cover major repairs and replacements of common areas over 25 years.

Mandatory content:

  • Based on the maintenance log.
  • Current fund balance.
  • Estimated annual costs for each repair or replacement.
  • Annual recommended contributions (including restricted common areas).
  • Explanation of the calculations.

Frequency: At least every 5 years. Annual review recommended.

Using the results:

  • The board must set fund contributions based on the study's recommendations.
  • Until the first study is obtained: minimum annual contribution of 0.5% of the reconstruction value.
  • The fund must be partially liquid, short-term, with guaranteed capital.

The attestation on the state of the co-ownership (art. 1068.1 C.C.Q.)

Objective: Inform the prospective buyer about the financial and physical condition of the co-ownership before purchase. Provided within 15 days of request.

Minimum mandatory content:

  • Current contingency fund balance and study recommendation for the current year.
  • Common expense contributions (required and paid) for the past 3 years.
  • Liquidity, surpluses or deficits for the last 3 fiscal years.
  • Current year budget forecast.
  • Confirmation of mandatory insurance + self-insurance fund amount and maximum deductible.
  • Summary of the past 5 years: inspections, claims, major repairs (date + cost).
  • Planned works for the next 10 years (date + estimated cost).
  • Ongoing litigation and amendments to the declaration of co-ownership over the past 3 years.

A free template is available on the website of the Société d'habitation du Québec (SHQ). The attestation must be dated and signed by a board member or by the manager.

3. Other important measures

  • Advances for new condominiums (art. 1791.1 C.C.Q.): Enhanced protection through trust, warranty plan, insurance or surety bond.
  • Transparency: The board must transmit minutes and resolutions more quickly.
  • Insurance: Increased obligations for the corporation.
  • Self-insurance fund: Specific rules on its use.

5. Who does what?

Corporation / Board

Orders documents, updates the log, sets contributions, keeps the registry and provides the attestation.

Unit owners

Pay mandatory contributions, have right of access to documents.

Developers

Responsible for initial documents in new condominiums.

Professionals

Engineers, architects, appraisers, technologists or CPAs — independent.

6. Practical consequences

  • Possible fee increase: To catch up on an undercapitalized fund (up to 10 years to reach the recommended level).
  • Unit value: A well-managed condominium (up-to-date log and study) sells better and at a better price.
  • Liability: Directors must justify decisions based on professional documents.
  • Non-compliance: Risk of lawsuits, difficulty selling and unit owner dissatisfaction.

Frequently asked questions about Bill 16

Everything condominium boards need to know about the reform of the Civil Code of Quebec.

What is Bill 16 for condominiums in Quebec?
Bill 16 (Bill no. 16, 2019, chapter 28) is a major reform of the Civil Code of Quebec that modernizes the management of divided co-ownerships. It requires condominium corporations to maintain a maintenance log, carry out a contingency fund study, and deliver an attestation on the state of the building at unit sales.
What are the three main obligations under Bill 16?
The three mandatory pillars of Bill 16 are: 1) The maintenance log, which records all past and planned work on the building. 2) The contingency fund study, carried out by an independent expert, which assesses the contributions required to fund future work. 3) The attestation on the state of the co-ownership, a document required at every sale of a co-ownership fraction.
What is the deadline to comply with Bill 16?
The implementing regulation has been in force since August 14, 2025. Condominium corporations must be fully compliant by 2028, according to the progressive timeline set out in the law. Obligations vary depending on the size and age of the building.
What is a maintenance log under Bill 16?
The maintenance log is an official register kept by the condominium corporation that documents the state of the building, maintenance work carried out, inspections completed and work to be planned. It must be updated regularly and is accessible to unit owners.
What is a contingency fund study for a condominium?
The contingency fund study is an analysis carried out by an expert (engineer, architect or professional technologist) that assesses the state of the building's components and calculates the contributions needed to fund major future work. It must be updated every five years.
What are the consequences if a corporation does not comply with Bill 16?
A corporation that is non-compliant with Bill 16 risks difficulty at unit sales (the attestation will be incomplete or unfavourable), disputes with unit owners and devaluation of the units. Buyers and their financial institutions increasingly require Bill 16 compliance before completing a transaction.
